Questions families shouldn't have to wonder about

When someone you love is living at home with the help of carers or regular visitors, there are questions that are hard to ask — but impossible to stop thinking about.

🕵️

Did the carer actually come?

You're paying for morning visits. Did they show up today? Were they on time? How long did they stay? You have no way to know without asking — and that feels awkward.

🚪

Who's been letting themselves in?

Several people have a key — a neighbour, a cleaner, a grandchild. Keeping track of who came when is impossible. And if something goes missing, there's no record to look back on.

😟

Were there any strangers?

Your loved one may open the door to anyone. If someone unfamiliar comes in, you won't find out until much later — if at all.

Teiker answers all of these questions — automatically.

Every person who enters the home is identified, logged, and — if unfamiliar — flagged immediately. You always know who came, when, and for how long.

How presence detection works

1

🪪 EVERY VISITOR IS RECOGNISED

Familiar faces welcomed. Unknown faces flagged.

When someone enters the home, Teiker recognises who they are. Carers, family members, neighbours and regular visitors are identified automatically. If Teiker doesn't recognise someone, you are notified straight away.

  • 👩‍⚕️ Carers: confirmed present, arrival and departure recorded.
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Family: your loved one knows a family visit is logged and they feel looked after.
  • 🏘️ Neighbours & regulars: recognised and logged without any alert.
  • ❓ Unknown person: immediate alert sent to family.

Frequently asked questions

How does Teiker recognise visitors?

Teiker uses facial recognition to identify people as they enter and leave the home. Known people — carers, family, neighbours — are registered once and recognised automatically from then on. No action is needed by the visitor or by your loved one.

What happens if someone new comes to the door?

If Teiker doesn't recognise the person, it immediately sends an alert to the designated family members, including the time of entry and whether the person is still inside. You can then decide how to respond.

How do we add a new person to the recognised list?

It takes just a moment through the Teiker app. Once added, that person will be recognised on all future visits and their comings and goings will be logged automatically — without any further alerts.

Can I see how long the carer stayed?

Yes. The visit log shows the exact arrival and departure time for every recognised person. You can access it at any time from the app, on any device.

Do carers or visitors need to do anything?

No. The system works automatically. Visitors do not need to press anything, sign in, or carry any device. Recognition happens quietly as they enter.

Who can see the visit log?

Only the people your loved one explicitly authorises. They remain in full control of who has access to their home's visit history.
