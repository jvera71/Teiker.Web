Why elderly people are the main target

Fraudsters deliberately target older adults. They know that trust, politeness and social isolation make them more vulnerable — and they exploit that shamelessly.

📞

Phone Scams

Fraudsters impersonate banks, government agencies or even grandchildren in distress to obtain money, card details or personal information — often over a single phone call.

"Grandma, I'm in trouble. Please don't tell Mum. I need £500 right now."

🚪

Door-to-Door Fraud

Fake utility workers, roofers or charity collectors gain entry to the home with forged credentials, then overcharge for unnecessary work or steal valuables while the owner is distracted.

"We're from the council. We noticed a problem with your roof — we'll need access right away."

💻

Online & Email Scams

Phishing emails, fake parcel notifications or prize-winning messages trick people into handing over bank details, passwords or making payments to accounts they believe are legitimate.

"Your parcel could not be delivered. Pay £2.99 to reschedule."

The most common scams targeting older adults

Teiker is specifically trained to recognise these fraud patterns and intervene at the right moment.

👴

Grandparent Scam

A caller poses as a grandchild in trouble — arrested, hospitalised or stranded abroad — and begs for an immediate bank transfer. The emotional urgency bypasses rational thinking.

Teiker detects: emotional distress language, requests for secrecy, urgent money transfer demands.

🏦

Bank Impersonation

Fraudsters call pretending to be from the victim's bank, claiming the account has been hacked. They instruct the person to transfer funds to a "safe account" — which belongs to the fraudster.

Teiker detects: account compromise claims, instructions to move money, requests for PINs or passwords.

🏗️

Rogue Trader / Doorstep Fraud

Bogus tradespeople knock on the door claiming the roof, driveway or drains need urgent repair. They charge vastly inflated prices for shoddy or entirely unnecessary work.

Teiker detects: unknown visitor flag, urgency language, cash payment requests.

❤️

Romance & Friendship Scam

A fraudster builds a warm relationship — by phone or online — over weeks or months, then invents a crisis requiring financial help. Victims are too embarrassed to tell their families.

Teiker detects: unusual frequency of contact, secrecy signals, repeated money-related conversation.

Frequently asked questions about fraud prevention

Does Teiker record phone calls?

No. Teiker analyses conversations in real time but does not record or store them. When a suspicious pattern is detected, only a brief flag and summary are saved — not the conversation itself. Your loved one's privacy is always protected.

What happens when Teiker detects a possible scam?

Teiker takes two immediate actions: it gently intervenes in the conversation to help your loved one pause and think, and it sends an alert to the family members you have designated. You can then call, visit or listen in — whatever feels right for your situation.

Will my loved one feel embarrassed or patronised?

No. Teiker frames its interventions as friendly reminders, not accusations. It might say: "Margaret, banks never ask for your PIN by phone — would you like to call the number on the back of your card to check?" This keeps your loved one in control and feeling capable.

Can I customise which alerts I receive?

Yes. You choose the sensitivity level and the types of alerts you want. You can opt for immediate notifications for every flag, or a daily digest if you prefer less frequent updates. Everything can be adjusted at any time.

Does this work if my loved one gets a call on their mobile?

Teiker monitors conversations that take place in the home environment. For calls made on a mobile in the house, Teiker can detect and flag suspicious patterns during the call. We recommend speaking with an advisor to find the best setup for your specific situation.
