Four dashboards, one complete picture

1

🏃‍♂️ Daily Activity & Energy

Everything you need to know about how much you moved today

This is your everyday dashboard. At a glance you can see how active you have been, whether you have hit your movement goals, and how many calories you have burned — both at rest and during exercise.

Metrics included
  • 👟 Step count — daily total and progress towards your personal goal
  • 🔥 Calories burned — active calories vs. total daily expenditure
  • ⏱️ Active minutes — time spent in moderate or vigorous movement
  • 📏 Distance covered — in km or miles, on foot or by any activity
  • 🏢 Floors climbed — vertical effort that many people overlook
  • 🪑 Sedentary time alerts — gentle reminders if you have been sitting too long
Teiker in action

"Good afternoon, Margaret. You have taken 4,200 steps so far — about halfway to your daily goal. A short walk after lunch would get you there nicely."

"You have been sitting for two hours. Ready for a five-minute stretch?"

2

🚶‍♀️ Mobility & Gait Analysis

Preventive health — spot changes before they become problems

How you walk says a great deal about your health. This dashboard is especially valuable for older adults, anyone recovering from injury or surgery, and family members who want early warning of mobility changes.

Teiker in action

"Margaret, over the past two weeks your walking speed has dropped slightly and your steps feel less even. This is worth mentioning at your next GP appointment."

"Your balance score today is 82 out of 100 — very good. Keep it up!"

Metrics included
  • 🚶 Gait speed — a clinically recognised indicator of overall health in older adults
  • 📐 Stride length & symmetry — detects limping or uneven walking patterns early
  • ⚖️ Balance score — derived from accelerometer data during normal movement
  • 🦶 Step cadence — steps per minute; changes can precede a fall
  • ⏳ Double support time — how long both feet are on the ground simultaneously
  • ⚠️ Fall risk index — personalised risk rating updated daily

Who benefits most: older adults living independently, people recovering from hip or knee surgery, anyone with a history of falls, and families who want reassurance without being intrusive.

3

❤️ Vital Signs & Recovery

How your heart, lungs and nervous system respond to daily life — and recover during sleep

This dashboard groups the metrics that reveal your body's internal state: how hard your cardiovascular system is working, how well your blood is being oxygenated, and how deeply you recover each night.

Heart & lungs
  • 💓 Resting heart rate — a long-term trend is one of the best fitness indicators
  • 📊 Heart rate zones — time in fat-burn, cardio and peak zones during activity
  • 🫁 SpO2 (blood oxygen) — flags low readings that may warrant medical attention
  • 🌊 Heart Rate Variability (HRV) — reflects autonomic nervous system balance and stress
  • 💨 Respiratory rate — breaths per minute; elevated rates can indicate illness early
Sleep & recovery
  • 🛌 Sleep stages — time in REM, deep, light sleep and awake periods
  • ⭐ Sleep score — a single number summarising night quality
  • 🧘 Stress index — detected from HRV patterns throughout the day
  • 🔋 Body battery / recovery score — how ready your body is for the day ahead
Teiker in action

"Good morning! Your resting heart rate last night was 58 bpm — that's your best reading this month. And you got 1 hour 20 minutes of deep sleep."

"Margaret, your SpO2 has been reading 93% for the past two nights, which is a little below your usual range. It might be worth mentioning to your doctor."

"Your stress levels have been higher than usual this afternoon. Shall I guide you through a short breathing exercise?"

⚠️ Important: Teiker presents these readings to help you stay informed. It never interprets them as medical diagnoses. Any reading that concerns you should always be discussed with your doctor or nurse.

4

⚖️ Physical Profile & Environment

The slow-changing metrics that give a long-term view of your body and surroundings

Some metrics change slowly — body composition, weight trends — and some depend on where you are: temperature, humidity, air quality. This dashboard brings them together so you can see the full picture.

Teiker in action

"Margaret, your weight has been stable for the past three weeks — well done for keeping on track."

"The air quality in your bedroom is high at the moment. Opening a window for a few minutes before bed would help you sleep better."

"Your skin temperature last night was slightly above your baseline — it could be nothing, but keep an eye on how you feel today."

Body metrics
  • ⚖️ Weight & BMI — tracked over time to reveal real trends, not daily noise
  • 💪 Body composition — body fat %, muscle mass and hydration where supported
  • 🌡️ Skin temperature — deviations from baseline can signal illness or hormonal changes
Environmental sensors
  • 🌤️ Ambient temperature & humidity — comfort and sleep quality depend on these
  • 💨 Air quality index — especially relevant for people with respiratory conditions
  • 📡 Other peripheral sensors — blood glucose monitors, smart scales and more

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to wear a device 24 hours a day?

No. Teiker works with whatever data your device captures. Wearing it during the day gives activity and mobility data; wearing it at night adds sleep and recovery metrics. The more you wear it, the richer the picture — but there is no obligation.

Can Teiker diagnose illnesses from my wearable data?

No. Teiker is not a medical device and never diagnoses anything. It presents your data clearly, highlights changes or patterns that may be worth discussing with a healthcare professional, and alerts you when readings fall outside your personal baseline. Any medical interpretation is always the responsibility of a qualified clinician.

Who can see my health data?

Only you, and the family members or carers you choose to share with. All data is encrypted in transit and at rest. Teiker never sells or shares your personal health data with third parties.

What if I do not own a wearable yet?

Teiker works perfectly without one — all the other features such as companionship, medication reminders and emergency detection still function fully. Adding a wearable simply unlocks this extra layer of health insight whenever you are ready.
