Words matter. Especially when you're not there.

Most of what happens in a home happens in conversation. How a carer speaks to your loved one, what topics are raised, whether the tone is respectful — these things are impossible to know without being present. Until now.

😔

Verbal mistreatment is hard to detect

Raised voices, belittling comments, threatening language — your loved one may not report it, out of fear, embarrassment, or simply because they don't want to worry you.

💶

Financial pressure and manipulation

Conversations about money, wills, gifts or property can be red flags. If someone close to your loved one is applying pressure, you need to know.

🤐

They don't always tell you everything

Your loved one may downplay situations to protect you, or simply forget to mention them. Audio analysis gives you an independent, objective record.

Teiker listens objectively, with no room for misremembering.

You define what concerns you. Teiker watches for it — and alerts you the moment it hears something that matches.

Three levels of audio awareness

Choose the level of detail that suits you. Each mode is independent — you can combine them or switch between them at any time.

1

🎙️ CONVERSATION TRANSCRIPT

A written record of everything that was said

During the hours you have configured, Teiker captures audio and converts it into a readable text transcript. You can review it at any time — a complete, timestamped record of conversations that took place in the home.

  • 📄 Full written record of everything said during active hours.
  • 🔍 Searchable: look for a specific word, phrase or topic in seconds.
  • 🕐 Timestamped: know exactly when something was said.
  • 📱 Always accessible from the app, on any device.
Sample transcript extract
09:14 am

"Good morning Margaret, how did you sleep? Let me help you get up."

09:15 am

"I slept alright thank you. My knee is a bit sore this morning."

09:22 am

"Let me know if you need anything. I'll start with breakfast."

Beyond the words, the conversation breakdown shows you who was talking and for how long. Without needing to know who each person is, you can see the shape of the conversation — was it balanced? Did one person dominate? Were there long silences?

  • 👥 Multiple speakers identified and separated automatically.
  • ⏱️ Speaking time per person — a quick indicator of conversation balance.

  • 📋 Combined with the transcript so you can read who said what in sequence.
3

🪪 NAMED SPEAKER RECOGNITION

Know exactly who said what

When speaker recognition is active, Teiker goes one step further: it recognises the voice of each known person and attaches their name directly to each line of the transcript. You don't just know what was said — you know exactly who said it.

  • 🎤 Voices registered for carers, family members and anyone who regularly visits.
  • 📛 Named transcript: each line clearly attributed to the person who spoke it.
  • ⚠️ Unknown voice alert: if an unregistered voice speaks, you are notified immediately.
  • 🔗 Linked to visit log: transcript and presence detection work together for a complete picture.

"Thank you dear. My knee is a bit sore today."

Frequently asked questions

Is audio captured around the clock?

No. Teiker only captures audio during the days and hours you have explicitly configured. Outside those windows, the microphone is completely inactive. You are always in control of when listening takes place.

Does my loved one know audio is being recorded?

Yes, always. Teiker operates with full transparency. Your loved one knows when audio capture is active, and they can ask Teiker to pause it at any time: "Teiker, I'd like some privacy." The microphone stops immediately.

Are the transcripts stored? For how long?

Transcripts are stored securely and encrypted, accessible only to people you authorise. You can configure how long they are kept, and delete them at any time. They are never shared or used for any purpose other than your family's oversight.

Can the carer or visitor hear the alert when something is flagged?

No. Alerts are sent silently to the family's devices. The person in the home is not notified that an alert has been triggered.

What if Teiker flags something that turns out to be innocent?

You can dismiss any alert and mark it as a false positive. Teiker learns from your feedback to reduce unnecessary alerts in the future, while remaining vigilant for genuine concerns.
