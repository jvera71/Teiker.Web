The dilemma families face

You want to know that your loved one is okay. But you also want to respect their privacy and independence. How do you protect them without making them feel watched?

😟

The Worry Side

You can't call them every hour. If they don't pick up, you worry. You don't know if they slept well, if they ate, if they fell. You live with constant anxiety.

"I ring twice a day and see them on Sundays, but I'm constantly worried during the week."

😤

The Privacy Side

Your loved one doesn't want cameras in every room. They don't want to feel watched. They've lived independently for 80 years and don't want to lose their privacy now.

"I'm not a prisoner. I don't want to live in a house full of cameras."

Teiker resolves this dilemma.

It monitors wellbeing automatically and discreetly without storing recordings, without invading private spaces, and always with their consent. Your family is informed. Your loved one keeps their independence.

Frequently asked questions about security

Is Teiker recording everything that happens at home?

No. Teiker does not record or store video or audio. It analyses in real time what is happening and only saves relevant information if an incident is detected. It does not spy — it protects.

Are there cameras in every room?

You decide where the devices are installed. Never in bathrooms or bedrooms without your explicit decision. And even in the rooms that have them, nothing is recorded continuously.

Can my loved one disable monitoring?

Yes. They have full control. They can ask Teiker to disable monitoring at any time and for any duration. "Teiker, I'd like some privacy for a while" — that's all it takes.

Who can see the information?

Only the people you authorise. The care home user decides who has access — which family members and at what level of detail.
